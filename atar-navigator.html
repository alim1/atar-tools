How this works

Step 1 — Raw to scaled. For each General/SEE subject, your raw score (0–100) is interpolated through QTAC's published 25th/50th/75th/90th/99th percentile breakpoints from the 2024 ATAR Report, Table 6. Applied subjects use the letter-grade to scaled mapping in Table 8. VET qualifications get a fixed scaled score by AQF level from Table 9 (Cert III = 39.11, Cert IV = 52.02, Diploma = 59.14).

Step 2 — Pick the top 5. The Tertiary Entrance Aggregate (TEA) is the sum of your best 5 scaled scores, subject to eligibility rules: you need a passing English (grade C or higher in any of English / EAL / Literature / English & Lit Extension / Essential English) but English only counts in your TEA if it's in your top 5. Precluded combos (e.g. you can't include Essential English with a General English) are filtered out. Source: Calculating the ATAR in Queensland, §4.

Step 3 — TEA to ATAR. We map your TEA to a percentile-among-ATAR-eligible-students using a calibrated curve, then read off the ATAR band from the 2024 ATAR Report's Table 14 cumulative student counts. We do not replicate QTAC's full cubic-spline participation model (its inputs aren't public) — expect ±2 to 3 ATAR points of variance vs. QTAC's official Navigator for the same input. This is an industry rule-of-thumb, not a QTAC-published figure.

What this tool can't see: school-based assessment context, future cohort variation, external exam variance. The estimate uses 2024 scaling data; 2025 cohort outcomes will differ.

Disclaimer. This is an estimator, not a prediction. Quoting QTAC directly: "This estimate is based on previous years' results and does not predict the ATAR that will be received in the future. QTAC does not endorse the use of ATAR Navigator estimates as a prediction of the ATAR." Source: QTAC ATAR Navigator.
